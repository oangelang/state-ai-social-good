The State of AI for Social Good

Updated continuously. Curated honestly. No hype welcome.

For the practitioners, funders, and technologists who need to know what has actually been tried — what worked, what failed, and where the real gaps are. Not a highlight reel. A field map.

Climate & Environment
Deployed
14
SDGs
13, 15
What's been tried
Google — Flood Hub
7-day riverine flood forecasts. 80 countries, 460M people covered. Pre-positioned Brazil crisis response 2024.sites.research.google/floodhub
Rainforest Connection
Acoustic ML detects chainsaws in forest canopy audio. Rangers get real-time phone alerts.rfcx.org
Climate TRACE
Satellite AI tracks facility-level emissions globally. Claims 99.9% of global emissions tracked.climatetrace.org
Pano AI
360° cameras + CV scan for earliest wildfire smoke signals. Deployed with first responders.pano.ai
Honest cautions
Flood models reach only ~1% of global watersheds. Communities with the least data bear the highest risk.
Carbon credit verification AI has been used to inflate offset claims. Same tech, opposite intent.
Most climate AI addresses mitigation. Adaptation — where the poorest need it — is mostly still at pilot stage.
Meta and Google ended their climate AI programs. Field sustainability without corporate backing is unresolved.
Open questions
Can AI for climate survive structurally without tech company funding?
Who owns training data from the communities bearing the most climate risk?
How do we evaluate tools that work in pilots but haven't survived a grant cycle?

Health & Medicine
Deployed
22
SDGs
3, 10
What's been tried
ARMAN — mMitra
Predicts dropout risk for pregnant women in rural health programs. Reached 2.9M women; 30% increase in high-risk engagement.armman.org
Zipline
Autonomous drones deliver blood + vaccines to remote areas. Millions of deliveries in Rwanda and Ghana.flyzipline.com
UNICEF OOI
AI identifies "zero-dose children" who've missed vaccinations from community health worker data.unicef.org/innovation
Spore.Bio
Biophotonics AI detects drug-resistant bacteria in under one hour vs. multi-day lab tests.spore.bio
Honest cautions
AI diagnostics trained on Western data perform worse in Sub-Saharan African clinical contexts. Dataset provenance matters.
Mental health chatbots without clinical oversight have caused documented harm. Escalation paths are not optional.
Most health AI requires connectivity + devices that community health workers in the field don't have.
Open questions
How do we build training datasets representing communities most affected by health inequity?
Who is liable when an AI health tool fails in a resource-constrained context with no fallback?
Can offline-capable diagnostic tools be built that function without connectivity?

Ocean & Water
Deployed
8
SDGs
6, 14
What's been tried
Global Fishing Watch
ML + satellite tracks millions of daily vessel positions. Near real-time global fishing activity map.globalfishingwatch.org
The Ocean Cleanup — Interceptor
CV identifies plastic debris in rivers via autonomous cameras. Millions of pounds removed.theoceancleanup.com
CETI Project
Acoustic classification to decode sperm whale communication and monitor marine health.projectceti.org
UNICEF — Groundwater Mapping
AI + remote sensing locates aquifers in drought regions where drilling is prohibitively expensive.unicef.org/innovation
Honest cautions
Detection works. Enforcement depends entirely on political will. Global Fishing Watch is public; action on it is not.
Coral reef monitoring ML requires baseline data most reefs don't have. You can't train on what was never measured.
Marine carbon removal verification is early. AI claims outpace independent verification capacity.
Open questions
Can AI close the ocean data gap before irreversible tipping points are crossed?
What role should fishing communities play in designing tools that monitor their waters?
How do we build open-source ocean data frameworks not owned by a single company or government?

Can voice-first, multilingual agricultural AI be built for low-literacy users at a cost nonprofits can sustain?
Where are the open-source crop disease models trained on Global South data?

The real risks

Full risk analysis →

A site about AI for social good that doesn't name the harms is a brochure, not a field resource. These six categories are documented, active, and inadequately addressed.

01
Bias & Discrimination
AI systems trained on historical data inherit and amplify existing inequalities. Hiring tools, credit scoring, medical diagnostics, and criminal justice have all produced documented discriminatory outcomes even after explicit debiasing.
55% of AI experts and 55% of the US public are highly worried about AI bias — the rare issue they agree on.Pew Research, 2025
02
Epistemic Risk
Deepfakes, synthetic media, and AI misinformation erode the shared factual basis of public life. AI impersonations scammed people out of $5.3 billion in 2025. A deepfake of a finance minister defrauded a retired doctor of her savings.
70% of AI experts are highly worried about AI-generated inaccurate information — the top shared concern across groups.Pew Research, 2025
03
Environmental Cost
AI's carbon and water footprint is growing faster than most climate commitments. Training GPT-3 evaporated 700,000 liters of freshwater. US data centers are projected to reach 12% of national electricity consumption by 2028.
AI carbon footprint: 32.6–79.7M tons CO₂ in 2025. Water: up to 764 billion liters.ScienceDirect, 2025
04
Economic Disruption
Job displacement is uneven and accelerating. The communities least able to absorb disruption bear the most risk. Productivity gains are concentrating at the top. Entry-level roles are declining fastest — the exact roles that build career foundations.
56% of US adults are very concerned about AI eliminating jobs. Only 25% of AI experts share that concern.Pew Research, 2025
05
Security & Warfare
AI is lowering the barrier for cyberattacks, biological and chemical weapon design, and autonomous weapons development. Facial recognition deployed in conflict zones has caused wrongful detentions. Biometric data from humanitarian crises has been misused by state actors.
AI deepfake-as-a-service financial fraud caused billions in losses in 2025. Documented state misuse of humanitarian biometric data in multiple conflicts.AIhub, 2026
06
Governance & Power
AI capability is concentrating in a handful of companies and governments. Regulation is fragmented and slow. The EU AI Act is the most comprehensive framework — enforcement begins August 2026. The US still has no federal AI law. 83% of AI investment in Africa concentrates in just four countries.
45 US states proposed AI bills in 2024. 31 enacted laws. Zero at the federal level.Cimplifi, 2025
