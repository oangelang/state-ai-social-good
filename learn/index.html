The field guide

Problem → method

The question practitioners keep asking: which kind of AI actually fits my problem? Six problem shapes, the AI method that matches each, and real deployments from the field. Click any row to see examples.

Problem shape AI method

I have past examples with known outcomes and need to predict at scale

Historical labeled data exists. The signal is in images, sensor readings, or tabular records.

Supervised learning + computer vision

Learns patterns from labeled examples. Scales to images and data no human team could process.

Conservation

Global canopy mapping

Meta + WRI used a self-supervised vision model on satellite imagery to map individual tree canopy height globally at 1m resolution — impossible at human scale.

Meta Sustainability Blog, 2024

Economic development

Poverty prediction from space

Stanford trained Convolutional Neural Networks on satellite imagery using nighttime light as a proxy label, predicting poverty across regions with no ground-level survey data.

Stanford News, 2016

I need to generate, translate, or have conversations with information at scale

The work involves language. Synthesis, summarization, triage, or direct communication with people — often across multiple languages.

Generative AI + LLMs

Generates, translates, and converses. Powerful for language-heavy workflows where scale or multilingual reach is the barrier.

Maternal health

Multilingual health messaging

Jacaranda Foundation fine-tuned LLMs for Swahili, Yoruba, and Zulu to deliver maternal health guidance via mobile — reaching populations excluded by English-only models.

Jacaranda Health

Humanitarian response

Field worker support tools

UNICEF tested multilingual translation models for humanitarian responders in DRC, and voice-first AI systems that allow queries via basic phone calls in low-connectivity areas.

UNICEF Venture Fund

I'm making repeated decisions and need to learn and improve as I go

No complete dataset upfront. The world gives feedback after each action. Waiting to collect enough data before acting isn't an option.

Bandits + reinforcement learning

Sequential decision-making under uncertainty. Balances exploring new options with exploiting what already works.

Public health

Maternal health outreach

Harvard used restless multi-armed bandits to allocate limited health worker calls across beneficiaries in India — learning in real time which messages worked for whom.

Harvard Teamcore

Urban mobility

Bike-share rebalancing

RL agents learn to reposition bikes across dock stations in real time, adapting continuously to demand patterns across a city without a fixed schedule.

arxiv, 2024 — Citi Bike NYC

I need to allocate limited resources against known constraints

There's a clear objective, real limits, and the combinations are too many to search by hand. The problem is logistics, not prediction.

Optimization

Solves a precisely formulated mathematical problem. Fast and exact when constraints are well-defined. The practitioner's job is to be precise about what's being maximized.

Healthcare

Kidney exchange

Integer programming finds the set of donor-recipient swaps that saves the most lives given biological compatibility constraints — a combinatorial problem humans cannot solve manually.

Stanford Impact Labs

Food systems

Food rescue routing

Optimization matches volunteers, perishable donations, and recipient organizations dozens of times daily — maximizing food rescued within time and capacity constraints.

AAAI 2020 — 412 Food Rescue

My problem lives in a network — spread, connection, or influence matters

The relationships between entities are as important as the entities themselves. Disease spreads along networks. So does information, behavior, and risk.

Graph methods + network analysis

Models entities and the relationships between them. Captures how things spread, cluster, or depend on each other across a connected system.

Public health

HIV prevention among homeless youth

Influence maximization algorithms identified the peer leaders in social networks of homeless youth most likely to spread HIV prevention information to hard-to-reach members.

Harvard Teamcore

Climate

Weather forecasting at global scale

DeepMind's GraphCast uses graph neural networks trained on 40 years of atmospheric data to forecast weather across the entire planet in under one minute.

Google DeepMind

The people I'm trying to reach or stop are watching me and adapting

My intervention changes behavior. An intelligent actor is observing my strategy and finding ways around it. The world is not passive.

Game theory

Models strategic interaction between actors who each respond to the other's moves. Assumes an intelligent opponent who is also optimizing.

Conservation

Anti-poaching patrols

PAWS uses Stackelberg security game models to randomize ranger patrol routes — so poachers cannot learn the pattern and avoid protected areas.

AAMAS 2014 paper (PDF)

Security

Airport security (ARMOR)

Deployed at LAX, game-theoretic models randomize checkpoint placement so that no predictable pattern can be exploited by an adversary watching over time.

AAAI 2008 paper (PDF)
